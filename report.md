## 2. Behavioral Relationships

### 2.1 Type–Task Relationships

Cross-tabulation analysis reveals strong associations between query type and task:

- **Factual queries → Learning (~93%)**  
- **Procedural queries → Learning (~69%), Barrier (~27%)**  
- **Comparative queries → Evaluation (100%)**  
- **Experiential queries → Mixed (Learning and Evaluation)**  

---

### 2.2 Interpretation

These relationships indicate that query types correspond to distinct user intentions:

- Factual queries are primarily used for knowledge acquisition  
- Procedural queries reflect action-oriented problem solving  
- Comparative queries are used for decision-making  
- Experiential queries support exploration and subjective evaluation  

---

### 2.3 Key Insights

- Search behavior is structured and predictable  
- Query type is a strong indicator of user task  
- Different query types reflect different cognitive processes  

---

## 3. Time Series Analysis

### 3.1 Query Volume Trends

- Activity increases from Week 1 to Week 3  
- Slight decline or stabilization in Week 4  

This suggests growing engagement or increasing task complexity over time.

---

### 3.2 Type Evolution

- Ideas/options queries dominate all weeks  
- Factual, na queries increase over time  

---

### 3.3 Goal Evolution

- Planning is consistently the most frequent goal  
- Decision-making remains stable  
- Motivational reasoning increases in later weeks  

---

### 3.4 Task Evolution

- Option-seeking, Learning remain the dominant task  
- Option-seeking behavior increases over time  
- Barrier-related queries peak in Week 2 and decrease afterward  
- Evaluation queries decrease after Week 3
- Na increased signifcantly after Week 2

---

### 3.5 Interpretation
The temporal patterns in query type, goal, and task reveal a clear shift in user behavior from exploration toward decision-making and execution. The dominance and continued growth of ideas/options queries indicate that users are primarily engaged in evaluating alternatives rather than simply acquiring information. While factual queries increase over time, this suggests that users revisit objective information to validate emerging decisions rather than using it only in early learning stages. At the same time, planning remains the central goal throughout, demonstrating that search behavior is consistently purposeful and goal-driven. The rise in motivational reasoning in later weeks further indicates a psychological transition, where users increasingly seek reassurance and justification for their choices. Task evolution reinforces this progression: users begin with learning and option-seeking, encounter barriers early (peaking in Week 2), and then move toward reduced evaluation and more stable behavior after Week 3, implying that decisions have largely been made. ?
